Income Tax Slabs for Salaried Individuals (2024-2025)

For the tax year 2024-2025, the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) has updated the income tax slabs for salaried persons. Here's a breakdown of the new slabs:

  • Up to Rs. 600,000: 0% tax (no tax payable)

  • Rs. 600,001 - Rs. 1,200,000: 5% tax on the amount exceeding Rs. 600,000

  • Rs. 1,200,001 - Rs. 2,200,000: Rs. 30,000 + 15% of the amount exceeding Rs. 1,200,000

  • Rs. 2,200,001 - Rs. 3,200,000: Rs. 180,000 + 25% of the amount exceeding Rs. 2,200,000

  • Rs. 3,200,001 - Rs. 4,100,000: Rs. 430,000 + 30% of the amount exceeding Rs. 3,200,000

  • Above Rs. 4,100,000: Rs. 700,000 + 35% of the amount exceeding Rs. 4,100,000

These progressive tax rates mean that as your income increases, the portion of your salary taxed at higher rates also increases.

Step-by-Step Example: Calculating Your Salary Tax

Suppose your annual salary is Rs. 1,500,000.

  1. The first Rs. 600,000 is tax-free.

  2. The next Rs. 600,000 (up to Rs. 1,200,000) is taxed at 5%:5% of 600,000 = Rs. 30,000

  3. The remaining Rs. 300,000 (from Rs. 1,200,001 to Rs. 1,500,000) is taxed at 15%:15% of 300,000 = Rs. 45,000

  4. Total tax = Rs. 30,000 (from the second slab) + Rs. 45,000 (from the third slab) = Rs. 75,000

So, your total annual tax would be Rs. 75,000, and your net annual salary would be Rs. 1,425,000.
